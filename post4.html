Working from Home? The One Thing That Changes Everything

2026-04-10 • 5 分鐘閱讀 • 作者：Sunny

Three months ago, I closed my laptop and walked into the living room.

My partner looked up and asked: "Done for the day?"

I paused. "I'm not sure."

That moment stopped me cold. I had forgotten what "off work" even felt like.

Remote work gave us flexibility. But it took something more important: the boundary.

No one forced us to stay online 24/7. We just never designed it any other way.

🧱 Office walls weren't just partitions — they were signals.
Walk in: work mode. Walk out: life mode.
When the walls disappeared, the signal disappeared too.

Rebuilding the boundary isn't about working fewer hours.
It's about giving your brain a deliberate switching point:

  • A fixed end time — non-negotiable, to-do list done or not
  • A closing ritual — change clothes, take a walk, brew tea. Something your body knows means: done.
  • A dedicated workspace — a corner counts, as long as it's only for work

⚡ How you work is how you live.

Clear boundaries make you sharper at work.
And when the day ends — you actually come home.

🏠 Working from home doesn't mean your home has to be a permanent office.

When did you last log off — for real?
